History

The Taj Mahals history begins in 1631, when Mughal emperor Shah Jahan ordered its creation following the death of his wife Mumtaz Mahal. His deep grief drove the project forward, mobilizing artisans, calligraphers, and craftsmen from across the empire. Construction formally began in 1632 under imperial supervision.

Over the next two decades, the site evolved into a monumental expression of Shah Jahans rule and personal loss. Historical records describe continuous labor involving thousands of workers and significant imperial resources. By 1653, the mausoleum complex was completed, marking one of the most ambitious undertakings of the Mughal era.
