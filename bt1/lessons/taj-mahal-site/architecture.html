Architecture

The Taj Mahals architecture blends Persian symmetry with refined Mughal aesthetic principles, creating a perfectly balanced spatial composition. Its central dome rises above four smaller chhatris, forming a harmonious vertical rhythm. Intricate pietra dura inlay work decorates the surfaces with precise floral patterns.

The complexs layout follows a strict charbagh plan, aligning structures along a north south axis for visual coherence. The minarets are slightly tilted outward to protect the mausoleum in case of collapse. Finely carved marble screens inside filter light gently, enhancing the monuments serene atmosphere.
